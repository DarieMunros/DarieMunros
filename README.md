When you work as a developer, remember that a company’s priorities are not the same as your own. 
Organizations exist to protect their interests, their legal position, and their reputation - sometimes at the 
expense of the individuals who work for them. This doesn’t mean every company is malicious, but it does mean
you should operate with professional caution. Treat your career and your reputation as assets
that you personally manage. Document your work, keep clear records of decisions, and avoid assuming 
that “good intentions” will protect you if something goes wrong.

## Safety Measures while you Work for Corporations

* Save important emails and written instructions.
* Link critical work accounts to recovery options you control when appropriate (personal accounts).
* Keep copies of key communications, approvals, and decisions so there is always a clear record of what was asked of you and who authorized it.
* Watch for monitoring tools such as keyloggers or unusual tracking software.
* Be aware that company machines may include monitoring tools. Understand what software is running so you know how your activity might be recorded.
* Regularly verify running processes on your company machine.
* Occasionally review active processes, installed programs, and system behavior so you can recognize anything unusual or unexpected.
* Treat corporate environments cautiously and protect your own records.
